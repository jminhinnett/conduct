## B. Responsibilities of Era of Health
**Short version**

*Era of Health manages you and your patient's Information in compliance with HIPAA's Security and Privacy Rules and U.S. Department of Health & Human Services (HSS).*

### 1. Disclosure of Information
Era of Health will not use or disclose you or your patient's Information in any way not covered in this Agreement or as required by law. 

### 2. Securing of Information
Era of Health will secure you and your patient's Information to prevent unauthorized use or disclosure, including implementing requirements of the HIPAA Rules.

### 3. Breach of Information
Era of Health will report to Your Organization any breaches of you or your patient's Information that are not covered in this Agreement.

### 4. Account of Information request
Era of Health will communicate when a patient requests an account of their Information and assist Your Organization in responding, completing or denying the request. 

### 5. Subcontractors compliance 
Era of Health will ensure that any subcontractors it may engage with that have access to you or your patient's Information will agree to the same restrictions and conditions that apply to the Era of Health with respect to you or your patient's Information.

### 6. Your HIPAA Rules Obligations 
Era of Health will carry out Your Organization’s obligation under the HIPAA Rules, require Era of Health to comply with the requirements applicable to the obligation.

### 7. Your Health & Human Services (HHS) Compliance
Era of Health will make available to HHS its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of protected health information received from, or created, or received by the Era of Health on behalf of, Your Organization for purposes of U.S. Department of Health & Human Services (HHS) determining Your Organization’s compliance with the HIPAA Rules.


## C. Responsibilities of Your Organization

### 1. Minimum Information to Era of Health 
Your Organization is responsible to provide Era of Health only the minimum Information necessary to accomplish the Services.

### 2. Follow HIPAA Rules 
Your Organization is responsible for using administrative, physical and technical safeguards at all times to maintain and ensure the confidentiality, privacy, and security of Information transmitted to Era of Health, in accordance with the standards and requirements of HIPAA Rules.

### 3. Disclose Your Organization changes affecting Era of Healths compliance with HIPAA Rules
In the event that Your Organization honors a request to restrict the use or disclosure of Information pursuant to Code of Federal Regulations 45 CFR 164.522(a) or makes revisions to its notice of privacy practices of Your Organization in accordance with Code of Federal Regulations 45 CFR 164.520 that increase the limitations on uses or disclosures of Information or agrees to a request by an Individual for confidential communications under Code of Federal Regulations 45 CFR 164.522(b), not provide Era of Health any Information that is subject to any of those restrictions or limitations to the extent any may limit Era of Health’s ability to use and/or disclose Information as permitted or required under this Agreement unless Your Organization notifies Era of Health of the restriction or limitation and Era of Health agrees in writing to honor the restriction or limitation. In addition, if those limitations or revisions materially increase Era of Health’s cost of providing services under the Agreement including this Agreement, Your Organization shall reimburse Era of Health for such increase in cost.

### 4. Obtain Consent
Your Organization must obtain any consent or authorization that may be required by local and national laws and regulations prior to furnishing Era of Health the Information for use and disclosure in accordance with this Agreement.

### 5. Request that violate HIPPA Rules
Your Organization cannot request Era of Health to use or disclose Information in any manner that would violate the Code of Federal Regulations 45 CFR Part 164 subpart E.

If either Party knows of a pattern or practice of the other Party that constitutes a violation of this Agreement then the non-violating Party shall provide written notice of the violation to the other Party that specifies the nature of the violation. The violating Party must correct the violation on or before thirty (30) days after receipt of the written notice. In the absence of a satisfactory correction to the non-violating Party within the specified timeframe or in the event the violation is reasonably incapable of correction, then the non-violating Party may terminate this Agreement.
